Manners and Morals

Manners and Morals

An Introduction

Friday, September 10, 2010

By George Moore

How do we raise our kids to be happy and good?

 

That’s a question that has been asked since people began asking what life is all about. As far back as we can remember, once people considered the meaning of life, they talked about how to bring up their children the right way, the way that would help them act in harmony with themselves and with other people.

 

It’s a vast subject. When you think about it, you realize that raising children has religious, moral, and social aspects. The greatest philosophers and religious teachers as well as grandparents and the shopkeepers down the street have joined the conversation.

 

The discussion is ongoing. It has to be worked out by each generation in each civilization, though luckily the generations overlap and share and pass on their values. Every parent joins in some way. Fortunately, children all become civilized. At least, we hope so!

 

What can I, George Moore, offer that hasn’t already been said better by someone else?

 

Well, my parents and children and brothers and sisters have ended up fairly decent and happy. I think my parents did a good job and my kids tell me they think theirs did. Though this doesn’t make me a credentialed expert, I’d like to share some thoughts.

 

Civilization in its social aspect consists of manners and morals. I value a civilization founded on compassionate consideration for others. Compassion begins with courtesy, and courtesy begins with good manners, so I will begin this column with manners. Over the coming months I hope to discuss parenting topics such as love, respect, training, encouragement, consistency, parental disputes, honesty, happiness.

 

Post your comments, suggest new topics, talk about what matters to you. Let’s have some fun, and together we may become better parents. I invite you to join the discussion!
