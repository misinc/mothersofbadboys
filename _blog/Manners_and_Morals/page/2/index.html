jeanne commented on 11-Nov-2009 04:27 AM
All my best friends were strangers at some point. At least be polite and acknowledge other people!
monda commented on 12-Nov-2009 11:12 AM
When we first moved to our town, about 15 years ago, I made a point of walking to downtown and parks with the kids. My 4 year old's observation was: "they don't like us cause they don't look at us", my 15 year old responded "they don't know how cool we are, yet!" - which was a comforting reasoning for the young one.

The truth is, I found over the years that residents of this small town have not been blessed with much cultural diversity. We do have darker complexion than most people here, still. And I may still sound as if I just got off the boat from overseas. We have been the token first generation foreigners in the school district or business circle. So I took it upon myself (and modeled for our kids) to establish contact with people who merely seemed uninterested or ambivalent about us. I reframed unfriendliness as their lack of knowledge. Approaching unfamiliar people with respect and smile did wonders. Acknowledging every single polite or nice gesture and encounter did help. Before long, we were invited to so many local functions that I couldn't fit into my schedule :o) People closer to us knew about our culture enough to ask for news updates or recipes from my motherland. And it felt Good!

Marco was correct back then - Now they know how cool we are.
